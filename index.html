About Me

I am a senior undergraduate at the University of Maryland majoring in GIS and Geology, and I find that the two fields of study compliment each other nicely. My professional interests are wide-ranging: I enjoy programming interactive web maps with open-source packages and getting out in the field to observe natural processes.

I started off studying just geology, but after being exposed to ArcGIS during an internship with the National Center for Smart Growth, I realized the potential there was to expand my skillset as an aspiring environmental professional through spatial analysis and mapping. From there, I began to take GIS classes and soon declared it as a second major.

It is my goal to find a career that has a healthy balance between earth science and GIS, and I believe my projects reflect that. Feel free to browse through them here.
